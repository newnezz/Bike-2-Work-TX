Texas rules for 15-year-olds (quick guide)
  • Minimum age: 14 for most non-farm jobs. Under 14 is very limited.
  • Work permit: Texas requires an Application for Child Actor/Performing Artist or Exemption Certificate (commonly called a work permit) from the Texas Workforce Commission for minors under 18 in many cases. Ask the school or employer how to get one before starting.
  • Hours (school in session): No work during school hours. Max 3 hrs/day, 18 hrs/week on school days. Work only 7 a.m.–7 p.m. (until 9 p.m. June 1–Labor Day).
  • Common OK jobs: Grocery/retail (cashier, stocking, bagging), fast food with limited cooking duties, movie theaters, bowling alleys, office/clerical work.
  • Usually NOT OK: Construction, warehouse, manufacturing, driving, operating power slicers/grinders, freezers/meat coolers, most hazardous work.
  • This app filters by business type — always confirm with the employer. Laws: TWC Child Labor · Federal FLSA
